Usability and Accessibility

Why?

Technological advancement has continued to aid our life. For instance, software, websites, and mobile applications help to reduce work in process and get things done faster and more convenient. To which we are able to adapt these technologies into a routine for their own convenience, such as checking bus timetables on a smartphone instead of checking at a bus stop.

However, some people with certain disability, such as colour blindness, hearing impairment, etc., may not receive a full experience from websites and find that it is difficult to use. Therefore, accessibility is also an important factor needed to be taken into account in the procedure of design, because this would be the easiest way to control an overview of software or website before it is produced.

Not only accessibility, but usability is also important to implement software application. The usability will help users be able to learn how to use the application on their own and understand how it works easily. This can increase engagement with the user and keep users stay longer on a software application.

From the BBC reported, most UK websites are still failing to meet the web accessibility standards and support people with disability (Rourke, 2017). This is due to the revenue loss because customers may not understand the subject that appears on the website, and user tend to close the website once they find it difficult to follow (Rourke, 2017). Moreover, both usability and accessibility can encourage conversion of website interface which is able to increase user experience (Bolton, 2017).
